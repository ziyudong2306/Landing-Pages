But before I get to that, let me explain why tucking your chin is the major cause of snoring.

Think about CPR training—the first thing rescuers do is tilt the victim's head back. 

Why? Because this instantly opens the airways. It's literally the difference between life and death.

When the chin is back, you get more air but your neck is strained.

When your chin is down, you get less air, and you snore.

With the chin in a neutral position, you improve airflow and posture.

The same principle applies to snoring. When your chin tilts downward:

  • Your tongue and jaw get pushed back
  • Your throat muscles collapse
  • Your airways narrow
  • You snore

And this happens no matter your weight and no matter how you sleep—whether you sleep on your back, side, or stomach!
